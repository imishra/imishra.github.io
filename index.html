Research Background
I am a PhD Student in computer Science at Virginia Tech and have been working in the area Natural Language Processing (NLP) with Dr. Lifu Huang. Though my primary research is focused in NLP, I have a diverse research background which includes compute vision and Re-inforcement Learning as well.

   Currenty, I am working on multi-model data understanding where I have developed a novel transformer architecture which can learn to reason acorss different modality of information (Table and Text). Our work also proposed a unique pre-training approach for training this transformer model which enforces the cross modality reasoning. The model does leverage the existing transformer model and proposes some new embedding layers to capture the tabular structure keeping the spatial relation intact.

    During my masters, we developed a novel framework that extended the sparse bayesian reinforcement learning to visual environment where an agent selects the best action based on the important visual snapshots from the past experiences. This frame was used to also explain the learning of a deep re-inforcement learning agent.
